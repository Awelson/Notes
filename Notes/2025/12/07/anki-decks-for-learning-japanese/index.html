    Anki decks for learning Japanese

    For those who don't know, Anki is a flashcard program which uses spaced repetition to help you learn and memorize stuff. It is especially popular with the Japanese language learning community for memorizing vocabulary and grammar. I have been wanting to study Japanese more seriously so I had to do some digging to figure out which of the many decks (set of flashcards) I should use, and I wanted to share some of what I found in this note.

    Vocabulary

    For vocabulary, there seems to be 2 main decks that people use : the JLPT Tango decks, and the Japanese Core decks.

    JLPT Tango

    also known as the Ankidrone Essentials. This consists of 5 subdecks based on JLPT level (N5, N4, N3, N2, N1) with sentence examples extracted from the JLPT Tango books (paste はじめての日本語能力試験 into the search bar). You can download / find more info about this deck by clicking here.

    Japanese Core

    This deck is based on the 6,000 most common Japanese words as per iknow.jp. The Core 2k deck contains just the first 2,000 words while the Core 2k/6k deck contains all 6,000. There is also a Core 10k deck which contains an extra 4,000 words (I am not sure where from).

    Variants

    There are many variants to the Tango and Core decks. For example, Core 2.3k and Kaishi 1.5k are (successfull) attempts at refining the original Tango and Core decks.

    Grammar

    The Dictionary of Japanese Grammar (DoJG) is basically regarded as THE bible for Japanese grammar. You can find an online version of it here as well as the associated Anki deck here.

    Making Your Own Deck

    After learning some few thousand words, a lot of people suggest moving on to building your own Anki decks. Personally, I can't be bothered but I looked into it anyway. The idea is : whenever you encounter a word you don't know and would like to learn (say while watching an anime), you can extract that word and the surrounding context and turn it into a flashcard; this is known as sentence mining which falls under the more general hood of immersion-based learning.
